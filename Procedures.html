How to Set Up a Trap

Step 1: Label Paper

Write your name and the date the trap was set up on the paper using a sharpie or pencil. You can find your trap number once you submit your trap in the Enter New Data tab on this website.

Step 2: Position the Trap

Place the paper inside the cup and the cup inside the stand. Use your foot to push the stand into the ground. If the ground is hard, you may need to use a hammer instead.

Step 3: Fill with Water

Fill the cup with water up to 2 inches below the edge of the paper. At this point, you can make appropriate modifications to the trap, such as adding materials into the cup.

Step 4: Enter Data

Go to the Enter Data tab of this website and select "I just set up traps." Fill out the form and submit your data.

Step 5: Label Trap Number

Navigate to the Active Traps List tab on this website and find the trap you just set up. Write the trap number on the paper. Check the trap every 3 days and refill the water as needed.

Step 6: Handle Mosquito Eggs

If you found eggs (check the Posts and Updates tab for help on identification), take out the paper and seal it in a plastic bag. If you suspect the eggs are Aedes eggs, please contact Dr. Blueford at blueford@msnucleus.org, and do not enter this data until you have confirmed that these eggs are Aedes. Otherwise, go ahead and enter the data using the Enter New Data tab.
