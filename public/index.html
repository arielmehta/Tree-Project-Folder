Our Vision

Our East Bay Hills have lost much of their biodiversity through grazing livestock and large scale deforestation which has rid the landscape of much of its native oak forests, riparian habitats, bunchgrasses, wildflowers and shrubery.

With this project, we aim to reforest 267 acres of the hills owned by the Masonic Retirement Home to utilize the 2 tons of food-waste produced by the home per week, increase habitats for native raptors, and other native wildlife incliding amphibians, mammals and reptiles, and also reduce the carbon footprint, while simultaneuosly educating the public and students about the environment.
